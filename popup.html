Information Page

GPA Calculator

How do I use this calculator?

The GPA Calculator is broken down into the the four high school grade levels. First, select the grade level using the nav-bar at the top of the screen. Next, you can enter any of the four terms from that grade level by clicking the dropdown.

Now, click the add course button to start inputting data. Enter your final grade from that course; either letter or percentage form. Enter the number of credits the class is worth, and check the box if the class is weighted. If you want to remove a course, click the remove button.

Your GPAs will be calculated in real time as you input data- no need for a submit button!

How does this calculator work?

The GPA calculations are based on official FCPS documentation. To learn more about how FCPS calculates GPA, visit this link, or search "Understanding GPAs" on the FCPS website.

How do I know if a class is weighted?

FCPS weighs Honors, AP, and IB classes the same. To check if your class is weighted, visit this link, or search for the FCPS High School Course Guide, and scroll to the bottom pages.

How can I save my current GPA data?

All of your inputted values are saved to your Google Account, meaning all the calculated GPAs are saved as well.

However, if you would like to download the calculated GPAs, click the download button next to the GPA table or at the bottom of this page. The data will be saved as a CSV file which you can use to import into Google Sheets or Excel.

Progression Chart

How do I use the Progression Chart?
It's easy! Calculated GPAs from the GPA Calculator are automatically transferred and graphed on the GPA Chart. Click the labels at the top of the chart to hide datasets and further analyze your GPA progression.

AI Assistant

How does the AI Assistant work?
The GPAi Assistant is a trained ChatGPT based chatbot capable of answering your academic questions. It is sourced and embedded from chatbase.co.
What questions can I ask the AI Assistant?
The GPAi Assistant is trained to answer questions regarding academic planning, work habits, college preparation, and more!
